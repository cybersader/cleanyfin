## What it is

cleanyfin lets a household **skip or mute** the parts of a movie or show it doesn't want — profanity, violence, nudity, and more — gated **per viewer profile**, with a per-title **"request a bypass"** escape hatch. The filtering data is **crowdsourced**: people tag segments while they watch, vote, and **share** their databases. No central authority — communities **federate** and follow the **curators** whose standards they share.

## The one rule that makes it legal

cleanyfin distributes **only timestamps + category metadata + edit-decisions** — never audio or video, and it never touches DRM. Filters apply in real time to the copy *you already own*, in *your own* player. That is the exact line U.S. law drew in the [Family Movie Act of 2005](https://www.copyright.gov/legislation/pl109-9.html): why ClearPlay is legal, and why VidAngel lost.
